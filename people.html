Shoshana Zuboff

Shoshana Zuboff is a Harvard professor who wrote the book “The Age of Surveillance Capitalism.” It looks at the development of digital companies like Google and Amazon, and suggests that their business models represent a new form of capitalist accumulation that she calls "surveillance capitalism". Big tech companies know more about us than we know about ourselves. Things that we never ever intended to disclose like political views, personality, emotions, sexual orientations, etc. These companies have been taking personal information and using it for their advantage and profit. This type of process is done in stealth under the ignorance of the population.

Edward Snowden

Edward Joseph Snowden who was a former National Security Agency (N.S.A) subcontractor turned into a whistle-blower(someone who exposes information or activity within a private, public, or government organization that is deemed illegal, or fraud). Edward Snowden was a computer expert and after leaving the N.S.A was a Central Intelligence Agency (CIA) employee and subcontractor. In 2013, he released confidential government documents to the press about the existence of government surveillance programs (a highly illegal "data mining" practice done by the N.S.A.)
